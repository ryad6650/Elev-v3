# Botanical Precision

## Philosophy

Botanical Precision draws from the tradition of 18th-century naturalist illustration — where scientific rigor met artistic devotion, where every leaf vein was traced with painstaking attention, where beauty emerged from disciplined observation. This movement treats functional icons not as utilitarian glyphs but as miniature specimens in a visual herbarium: each one meticulously crafted, as though a master engraver spent countless hours reducing a complex idea to its most essential, elegant form.

## Form & Space

Every shape begins with the circle — the seed, the weight plate, the plate of food, the zero-point of all measurement. From this origin, forms grow outward with organic confidence but geometric restraint. Lines are confident and singular: no redundant strokes, no decorative excess. Negative space is as deliberate as positive mark — the silence between elements carries as much meaning as the elements themselves. Each icon must feel like the product of deep expertise, where every curve was debated and every angle resolved through patient iteration.

## Color & Material

The palette is drawn from the earth itself: warm cream grounds (#f2e8d5), deep forest greens (#1b2e1d, #74bf7a) as living accents, and stone browns (#4a3728) for structural weight. Color is never decorative — it is informational. A single accent hue signals interaction; muted tones signal rest. The icons exist as if pressed into handmade paper with botanical ink, carrying the warmth of analog craft within a digital surface. This chromatic restraint demands master-level execution — when you have only two colors, every value must be perfect.

## Scale & Rhythm

Icons share a unified optical weight — no single glyph dominates or recedes. The rhythm across the navigation bar should feel like five specimens mounted at equal intervals in a museum vitrine: each distinct in silhouette, yet unified in density, stroke weight, and spatial presence. Corners are softly rounded like worn stone, never sharp, never purely mechanical. The proportional relationships between thick and thin strokes are calibrated with the painstaking attention of a typographer refining a single letterform across months.

## Composition & Identity

Each icon is a self-contained composition — a tiny world that communicates instantly at 24px yet rewards examination at 10x scale. The silhouettes must be unmistakable even as solid fills: Séances (movement, muscles, repetition), Poids (gravity, balance, measurement), Accueil (shelter, center, origin), Nutrition (nourishment, portions, sustenance), Historique (time, accumulation, pattern). The conceptual thread is the body itself — these five icons together map the athlete's daily practice like anatomical plates in a training codex, each one the product of countless hours of refinement by someone at the absolute top of their craft.
