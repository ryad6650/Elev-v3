# Warm Craft

## Philosophy

Warm Craft is the visual language of objects held in afternoon light — where every line carries the warmth of terracotta, where functional glyphs become small acts of craftsmanship. This movement treats navigation icons not as cold utilitarian symbols but as hand-drawn specimens from an artisan's notebook: each stroke deliberate, each curve resolved through patient refinement, each negative space as considered as the mark itself. The result must appear as though a master illustrator spent countless hours distilling complex ideas into their most essential, warm, and human forms.

## Form & Line

The primary instrument is the stroke — consistent in weight, softly rounded at terminals, never sharp or mechanical. Every icon begins from a unified grid but breathes beyond it with organic confidence. Corners are radiused like worn wood; joints are clean but never clinical. Stroke width is generous enough to read at 24px yet refined enough to reward examination at scale. Each form is meticulously crafted with painstaking attention to optical balance — a dumbbell's symmetry, a house's protective geometry, a fork's elegant taper.

## Color & Warmth

A single color dominates: warm brown (#4A3728), the color of aged leather and roasted coffee. This is not black — it is earth, it is warmth, it is approachability. Against transparency, these strokes float with the lightness of ink on handmade paper. The accent variant (#a0785c) appears only for active states, a golden-brown that suggests sunlit emphasis without departing from the family. This chromatic restraint demands master-level execution — when you have one color, every weight and spacing must be perfect.

## Rhythm & Unity

Across the navigation bar, icons share identical optical weight — no glyph dominates or recedes. They sit as a family: five siblings with distinct personalities but unmistakable kinship. Stroke weights, corner radii, and spatial density are calibrated with the precision of a typographer balancing a font family. The set feels like it was labored over as a unified collection, not assembled from disparate sources.

## Spirit

These icons carry the spirit of the Élev dashboard: warmth over coldness, craft over mechanism, invitation over instruction. They belong to the cream-colored world of DM Serif Display italics and soft gradients — artifacts from a design practice that values beauty in utility, that treats every 24px square as worthy of the same care as a gallery piece.
